MegaCampus: A Year-long Visual Dataset Capturing Seasonal and Elevational Variations

Xijun Liu, Yifan Zhou, Cheng Peng, Rama Chellappa
Johns Hopkins University

MegaCampus, a multi-appearance, multi-view, multi-elevation in-the-wild real-world dataset.

Abstract

We introduce MegaCampus, a multi-appearance, multi-view, multi-elevation in-the-wild real-world dataset. It depicts a comprehensive and expansive outlook of the Johns Hopkins University Homewood Campus. The images were captured and selected meticulously around the buildings within the campus, and were taken in including different seasons, at distinct time within a day, under wide-ranging weather conditions, at varying altitude, with manifold camera devices over a year-long time span.

Data Collection

The dataset consists of 10 individual buildings, covering lighting conditions including sunny, cloudy, and low-light at varying altitudes across four seasons. With each setting, a video was taken by circumnavigating around the building, ensuring full coverage of its exterior. After sampling the frames of the video and censoring the PII information, we obtained clusters of images. Then, we registered all images of individual buildings.

Alignment

Airborne images captured by a drone offer a wide perspective and less coverage by plants and transient objects. All images taken during the summer at an altitude of 60 meters above ground level were registered into a unified coordinate system, which serves as the anchor for subsequent data alignment and analysis. Then, we perform Procrustes alignment to align all images into the unified coordinate system. Here we show a video of the sparse reconstruction of the JHU Campus.
